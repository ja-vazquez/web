Research

News  

My current research focuses on Data analysis, mainly on the Lyman-α forest observed through BOSS; also on the parameter estimation and model selection of different Dark Energy and Inflationary models. In the past, I worked with dynamical systems in cosmology and scalar fields as the main candidates of being the Dark matter of the Universe. Some of my papers on progress can be accessed from links below.


PhD Thesis
“Constraining alternative cosmological models with current and future observations”
This thesis presents a Bayesian analysis of cosmological models in the light of the currently available data and forecasts for the next generation of experiments.

About

Jose-Alberto Vazquez


Research Associate
Brookhaven National Lab
US Department of Energy

My current research focuses on the data analysis of the Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS), as part of the BOSS and eBOSS collaboration. I am also interested on study the nature of the dark energy, as the main candidate to explain the accelerated expansion of the universe.
